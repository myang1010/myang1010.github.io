Some benefits from this session,

What's User Experince Design?

"User experince encompasses all aspects of the end-user's interaction with the company, its services, and its products."

User Experince(UX) desgin is the process desgin teams use to create products that provide meaningful and relevant experinces to users. This involves the design of the entire process of acquiring and integrating the product, including aspects of branding, design, usability and function.


  • Create meaningful experiences

  • UX design cycle

How is UX Importat

  • Connects users to your product
  • Better brand awareness
  • Increase revenue growth/business success
  • Can change behavior on a massive scale
  • Look at Apple, Amazon, Netflix, etc.

The Trends in 2021

  • Designing for virtual experinces with AR and VR
  • Evolution of computational design and AI
  • Focus on inclusive design
  • Data and design integration
  • Specialized UX professionals

Code Cafe: Hosted by Code Cafe Coding and Tech at Indianapolis Public Library

If you look carefully you could see the "Talking: Henry Herbst-Indy PL" text on the upper right corner. That's the Librarian Henry Herbst from Indianapolis Public Library who was giving the class.

It's inetresting to learn some free and easy to use photoshop tools. In this session the librarianin introduced two awesome tools, one is Pixlr, and the other one is Photopea. They are free, online alternatives for image creation and editing.

Why not Photoshop? Because Photoshop is incredibly expensive! It requires a monthly subscription fee in order to use and a lot of unnecessary features for most

Some Common Terms:

Canvas
the window that holds an image
Layer
each one is like a separate sheet of paper; the one on top obscures the one below
Crop
trim a portion of an image
Filter
automated way to alter an image (like blurring or coloring it)

Pixlr

  • More suited for image-editing and collages
  • User-oriented; offers templates and stock images
  • Two versions! Both are online. Both are free.
  • Pixlr X for beginners, simplified and intuitive
  • Pixlr E for more experienced users who want better control

Here is the Pixlr Resources: Pixlr FAQ

Photopea

  • Similar functionality as Photoshop
  • Many of the filters and options of Photoshop
  • Including the ability to adjust lighting and color
  • Compatible with a wide variety of image files

Here is the Photopea Resources: Learn Photopea and Tutorials

Personally I like Photopea better.
